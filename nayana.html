NaYaNa - A Phonetic Script

Making Communication Easy

NaYaNa was a new phonetic script, created by Dr. Nagarjuna G. and Mr. Vickram Crishna, Homi Bhabha Centre for Science Education, Tata Institute of Fundamental Research, Mumbai, which was based on the complete range of vocalisations currently accepted internationally as an ability of all human beings. Z@Z is read as NaYaNa, same as N@N.

This script was designed with some principles to make the script easy to read, although it potentially matches the full capabilities researched and documented as the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA). The main motivation for designing this script was to make writing dyslexia friendly.

Phonetic transcription (also known as phonetic script or phonetic notation) is the visual representation of speech sounds (or phones). The most common type of phonetic transcription uses a phonetic alphabet, such as the International Phonetic Alphabet.
